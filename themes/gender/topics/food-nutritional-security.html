Food and Nutritional Security

A significant implication of climate change and the resulting decline in agricultural production and food security in many parts of the world is that women are playing an increasingly important role in provisioning food for the family.

Lessons Learnt

  • Acquisition of land and other productive assets (e.g. equipment, technical inputs) can be facilitated through collective arrangements, especially when individual land tenure for women is constrained by social norms and customary practices.
  • Given that women/girls with higher levels of education are more likely to access markets and earn better incomes, investing more in education can lead to enhanced adaptive capacities.
  • It is essential to remove barriers for access to and management of finance and markets for rural women.
  • Climate change adaptation projects need to invest in time-saving technology so that women of all age groups have more time to devote to productive activities.
